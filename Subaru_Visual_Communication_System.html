The Core Concept

This system applies Turnberry Solutions' professional layout, typography, and information architecture (the "chassis") with Subaru's identity layered as accents and highlights—not dominant chrome. Like a McKinsey engagement deck: our rigor and structure, your brand and context.

What This Means

  • Turnberry is the foundation: White canvas, sans-serif typography, clean card patterns, professional structure
  • Subaru is the accent: Blue accent bar at the top, navy headings, accent borders on cards, logo reference where appropriate
  • Not both competing: Eliminates visual confusion from dual branding; instead, Subaru reinforces professionalism
  • Consistent across formats: HTML, PPTX, PDF, XLSX, email, and Miro all apply the same layering principle

Format-Specific Guidance

HTML (Web Deliverables)

  • Self-contained: All styles inline within <style> tag; no external CSS files
  • Interactive: Support filters, tabs, expandable sections; JavaScript for interactivity
  • Full design implementation: Color swatches, live type scales, component demonstrations (as you see in this document)
  • Responsive: Mobile-first media queries; readable on tablet and phone

PPTX (PowerPoint)

  • Slide layout: White background with Subaru Blue title text
  • Accent bar: 4px navy bar at top of each slide, or navy accent border on title placeholders
  • Typography: Helvetica Neue or Arial; title 24–28pt, body 12–14pt
  • Callouts: Use service lane colors for emphasis blocks and accent shapes
  • Footer: Client name (Subaru), date, slide number in 10pt gray text

PDF (Formal Reports)

  • Report layout: Clean reportlab output with white canvas and navy headings
  • Accent bar: 4px Subaru Blue bar at top of first page, then navy h2 headings throughout
  • Typography: Helvetica; heading 20pt bold, body 11pt regular
  • Graphics: Full-color charts and diagrams; accent colors from functional palette
  • Footer: Gradient footer bar matching header pattern on final page

XLSX (Dashboards & Workbooks)

  • Header row: Navy background (#00205B) with white text; 12pt Helvetica
  • Column headers: Navy text, light gray background for clarity
  • Data rows: Alternating white and very light gray (#F9FAFB) for readability
  • Status cells: Service lane colors for lane identification, status badges (e.g., green for On Track)
  • Pivot tables: Maintain navy headings and service lane color accents

Email (Campaign & Notification)

  • Layout: Table-based HTML; navy border-top accent (3–4px) on outer container
  • Header: White background, navy text for subject/greeting
  • Call-to-action: Navy button (Subaru Blue) with white text
  • Footer: Gray text in 11pt; include company name and link to main dashboard
  • Service lane accent: Colored left border (4px) on content cards if embedding workstream context

Miro (Whiteboard & Planning)

  • Sticky notes: Use best-available colors matching functional palette; navy for Exec, blue for Team Lead, orange for Contractor, etc.
  • Shapes: Rectangles for cards (white fill, navy 2px border), circles for milestones in service lane color
  • Text: Helvetica/Arial; 12pt for stickies, 14pt for titles
  • Connectors: Navy lines (2–3px) for critical path; gray for supporting flows
  • Accent blocks: Navy rectangles as section headers, gradient bars for timeline divisions

Internal Documents

When authoring internal Turnberry documents (not client-facing), swap the accent color:

  • Accent color: Amber (#E67E22) instead of Subaru Blue
  • Headings: Amber text or accent bars
  • Footer: Amber gradient bar
  • Rationale: Allows team to quickly distinguish internal workspaces from client deliverables

Key Principles for Consistent Application

1. Hierarchy: Professional First, Brand Accent Second

Turnberry's clean layout, clear typography, and card-based structure establish trust and readability. Subaru's blue accents reinforce brand identity without overwhelming the message.

2. Functional Colors Are Context, Not Brand

Training, Customer, Delivery, Frameworks, etc., are independent of both Subaru and Turnberry colors. They communicate engagement role and status.

3. Consistency Across All Formats

The same color palette, accent pattern (4px bar, gradient footer), and typography scale apply to PPTX, PDF, XLSX, email, and Miro. No custom layouts per format.

4. Accessibility & Clarity

Sufficient contrast between text and background. Functional colors chosen for Miro compatibility and color-blind readability. Service lane colors do not rely on red–green distinction alone.

5. Internal vs. Client Deliverables

Use Subaru Blue (#00205B) accents for client-facing work. Use amber (#E67E22) accents for internal Turnberry documents. This distinction enables quick visual scanning.
