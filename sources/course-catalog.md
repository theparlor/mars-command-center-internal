## Category: Foundations

Core agile concepts for consultants supporting client transformations. Understand why agile and product thinking matter, how to identify barriers, and develop strategies to support organizational change.

### Agile Mindset

**Week:** Wk 1
**Phase:** Phase 1
**Format:** Workshop
**Duration:** Half day
**Audience:** All teams (x2 rooms)
**Materials Status:** Ready — Deck + Handout + Worksheets

**Description:**
While many organizations use Scrum, many organizational structures hold them back into traditional development modes. Teams learn how agile development creates better business outcomes by enabling incremental learning and delivery. They work together to identify organizational barriers that inhibit adoption of Agile principles.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify how incremental delivery could benefit organizations
- Identify organizational barriers to Agile Manifesto principles
- Compare traditional and agile mindsets
- Identify potential mitigation strategies to the natural human resistance to continuous improvement

**Agenda:** Value of Agility · Agile Manifesto · Agile Mindset · Continuous Improvement

---

### Product Mindset

**Week:** Wk 11
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Format:** Workshop
**Duration:** ~2 hrs
**Audience:** All teams
**Materials Status:** Ready — Deck + Generated Content

**Description:**
Teams often spend months building wrong solutions because they jump straight from company strategy to delivery. Participants learn how a product mindset addresses those problems and explore how their organization could move closer to a product mindset. They explore how product vision and strategy keep teams focused on customer problems rather than solutions.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between project and product approaches when making decisions
- Apply product principles to identify improvement opportunities in their context
- Describe how vision and strategy connect and their importance in product development
- Recognize the value of product discovery and its impact on product development

**Agenda:** Projects vs Products · From Project to Product · Product Management Vacuum · Product Discovery

---

### Navigating Change

**Week:** Wk 13
**Phase:** Phase 4
**Format:** Workshop
**Duration:** ~2 hrs
**Audience:** All teams
**Materials Status:** Ready — Deck + Handout

**Description:**
Resistance to change is a normal part of the human condition. This course helps teams identify concrete steps for engagement. Participants reflect on where they can become owners of the change and help move the change initiative forward. They practice converting areas of concern into positive action and increased engagement.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize resistance to change as a natural response
- Identify change agent archetypes and the value each brings to navigating change
- Explain the levels of change engagement and benefits of increased engagement
- Identify opportunities for team ownership during organizational change

**Agenda:** Resistance to Change · Change Archetypes · Change Engagement · Change Ownership

## Category: Delivery

Essential planning and execution methods — adaptive roadmaps, customer-focused user stories, and flow optimization that deliver better business outcomes.

### Estimation

**Week:** Wk 5
**Phase:** Phase 2
**Format:** Workshop
**Duration:** ~2 hrs
**Audience:** All teams
**Materials Status:** Gap — Needs development

**Description:**
Effective estimation enables teams to forecast delivery, plan sprints accurately, and communicate realistic timelines. Participants learn relative sizing techniques and how to estimate team capacity considering velocity, sprint capacity, and availability factors.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand the purpose of estimation in agile planning
- Apply story pointing techniques including relative sizing, Fibonacci sequence, and T-shirt sizing
- Calculate and interpret team velocity
- Estimate sprint capacity accounting for team availability
- Use capacity-based planning to forecast delivery timelines

**Agenda:** Why We Estimate · Story Pointing · Relative Sizing · Fibonacci & T-Shirt Sizing · Team Velocity · Sprint Capacity · Availability & Planning

### User Stories & Backlog Fundamentals

**Week:** Wk 7
**Phase:** Phase 2
**Format:** Workshop
**Duration:** Half day
**Audience:** All teams
**Materials Status:** Ready — Deck + Handout + Slide Export

**Description:**
User stories that contain dependencies, are too big or have unclear test criteria slow teams down. Participants will learn to identify when stories fail to meet INVEST criteria. They will practice splitting stories, writing them to focus on value, and writing testable acceptance criteria. Combined with backlog structure, prioritization techniques, and refinement cadence.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize warning signs that stories violate INVEST criteria
- Apply INVEST to evaluate if user stories are ready for development
- Break down large user stories into smaller, manageable pieces
- Write story descriptions that clearly communicate intent
- Write testable acceptance criteria that define story completion

**Agenda:** INVEST · Splitting Stories · Descriptions · Acceptance Criteria · Backlog Structure · Prioritization · Refinement Cadence

### Achieving Flow

**Week:** Wk 11
**Phase:** Phase 3
**Format:** Workshop
**Duration:** ~2 hrs
**Audience:** All teams
**Materials Status:** Ready — Deck + Handout + Notes + Worksheets

**Description:**
Teams get stuck in cycles where senior members become bottlenecks while junior members wait on sidelines. Through hands-on exercises, participants experience how small batch sizes, limited WIP, focusing on overall flow, knowledge sharing, and self-organization reduce these problems.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Understand why smaller batch sizes create faster time to value and reduce risk
- Articulate the productivity loss due to multi-tasking
- Explain the importance of prioritizing flow over individual utilization
- Articulate the importance of knowledge sharing
- Describe the benefits of self-organizing teams

**Agenda:** Batch Size · Work in Progress · Shared Learning · Self-Organizing Teams

## Category: Frameworks & Practices

Foundational frameworks including Scrum, Kanban, roles & responsibilities, and team dynamics for effective product development.

### Scrum Fundamentals

**Week:** Wk 1
**Phase:** Phase 1
**Format:** Workshop
**Duration:** ~2 hrs
**Audience:** All teams (x1 room)
**Materials Status:** Ready — Deck + Handout + Worksheets (Subaru version)

**Description:**
Many teams struggle with Scrum because they don't understand the principles that make it work. Through hands-on exercises, participants build foundational competency in Scrum's structure: the roles, events, artifacts, and the empirical principles that guide effective product development.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Appreciate the value of empiricism in fostering a learning-oriented team environment
- Recognize the importance of Scrum values to a team's success
- Understand the structure and organization of the Scrum Framework
- Grasp the meaning and purpose of Scrum roles, ceremonies, and artifacts
- Explore Scrum Anti-Patterns and how to address them

**Agenda:** Foundations of Scrum · Scrum Roles · Scrum Ceremonies · Scrum Artifacts · Review

### Scrum Roles & Responsibilities

**Week:** Wk 1
**Phase:** Phase 1
**Format:** Interactive
**Duration:** ~90 min
**Audience:** All teams + leaders
**Materials Status:** Ready — Deck + Stances Reference

**Description:**
Teams often struggle when Product Owners become gatekeepers, Scrum Masters turn into project managers, or Developers wait for assignments. Through interactive exercises, participants explore behavioral patterns that distinguish effective Scrum roles from common dysfunctions.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Distinguish between effective patterns and anti-patterns for POs, SMs, and Developers
- Identify how roles can support each other in adopting effective patterns
- Experience the difference between manager-led and self-managing team dynamics

**Agenda:** Self-Managing Teams · Product Owner Patterns · Scrum Master Patterns · Developer Patterns
