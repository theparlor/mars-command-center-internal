📘 Training — Week 1 Recap & Week 2 Look-Forward

Covers: Agile Academy sessions delivered, observations, what's next

Week 1 Recap (Mar 2–6)

Week 1 was a full in-person training week at Camden. The team delivered four sessions across two parallel rooms to maximize coverage across all six in-scope teams. Attendance was strong and engagement was high — individual contributors demonstrated genuine curiosity and willingness to question current practices, which is a healthy signal this early.

  • Agile Fundamentals & Mindset — Delivered twice (two rooms). Established shared vocabulary around the "why" of agile, product thinking, and iterative delivery. Several participants connected the content to friction points they experience today, particularly around handoff delays and scope ambiguity.
  • Achieving Flow — Single session. Introduced WIP limits, pull-based systems, and the relationship between utilization and cycle time. The "highway at 100% capacity" analogy resonated strongly. This will be reinforced during embedded coaching.
  • Scrum Fundamentals — Single session. Covered roles, events, and artifacts using modern Scrum Guide (2020+) language. Notable: teams are already running some form of sprints but with inconsistent event structures and unclear role boundaries.
  • Kanban Fundamentals — Cancelled. This was a scheduling decision to make room for the newly added Roles & Responsibilities session (Dean, Louise, Brien, Turnberry consultants). Kanban deep-dive / Flow optimization is rescheduled to Week 11 as part of the "Flow / WIP + Product Mindset" advanced track.
Key Observation: Teams are enthusiastic but leadership engagement during training was uneven. Some managers attended portions; others delegated entirely. The leader readout this week is an opportunity to close that gap and ensure managers internalize the same language their teams are now using.

Week 2 Look-Forward (Mar 9–13)

  • Leader Readout — Brien and Dean will present a Week 1 learnings debrief to Greg, Gino, and platform managers. The goal is to share what we observed, what resonated, where gaps exist, and what coaching will target first.
  • No new formal training sessions this week — The focus shifts to coaching, observation, and relationship-building. The next training block (Estimation: Story Pointing & Capacity Planning) is Week 5.

Upcoming Training (Weeks 3–7)

Weeks 3–4 are coaching-focused with no classroom training (observation, relationship-building, coaching only). Week 5 brings Estimation: Story Pointing & Capacity Planning (2hr, all teams) — this is the only training that week. Week 7 brings two individual sessions: User Story Writing and User Stories & Backlog Fundamentals, run per-team (1hr each). These sessions build the practical scaffolding teams need before the Pre-Planning milestone at Week 6.

🧭 Coaching — Cohort Launch & Embedded Rhythm

Covers: Cohort assignments, coaching approach, Week 2 focus

Cohort Structure

Coaching is organized into two cohorts to ensure coverage depth and relationship continuity. Each cohort has a lead coach and a support resource for coverage and complementary perspective.

  • Cohort A — Dean Gabbert (Lead Coach), Louise Teeter (OCM Lead + coverage). Teams: Natalie, Marc, Kadeem.
  • Cohort B — Brien (Lead Coach), Jason Lindmeyer (coverage, remote some weeks). Teams: Gino's team, Kevin McCracken (Subaru.com), Vladimir Goldsteyhn (QA), Sam Sharkay (DevOps).

Week 2 Coaching Focus

This is the "observe, learn, establish trust" week. Coaches will attend team events, sit alongside teams during their regular work rhythm, and start building the relationship foundation that makes coaching effective. No corrections, no recommendations — just presence, curiosity, and pattern recognition.

  • Office Hours Q&A — Launching daily this week. Staffed by Dean and Brien. Open to any team member. This creates a low-stakes venue for questions that don't fit neatly into formal training or stand-ups.
  • Event observation — Attending each team's existing events (stand-ups, refinement, retrospectives if they run them) to catalog behaviors, language, and dynamics.
  • Manager 1:1 discovery — Continuing discovery conversations started in Week 0. Dean and Brien connecting with Gino, Kevin, Kadeem, Marc, Vlad, and Sam to understand their individual challenges, aspirations, and the political landscape they navigate.
Coaching Philosophy This Phase: "Earn the right to coach before coaching." The biggest risk in early engagement is moving to solutions before understanding the system. Week 2 is about absorbing, not prescribing.

📊 JIRA / Metrics — Environment Assessment & Baseline

Covers: JIRA current state, baseline data, Pulse Check launch

JIRA Workstream

JIRA onboarding and environment assessment kicked off during Week 1 with initial access setup. This week, Jason and Dean begin guided JIRA deep-dives team-by-team — documenting the current state of boards, issue types, workflows, and field usage. The goal is to understand what teams are doing now before introducing any standardization.

  • Map current board configurations and workflow states per team
  • Identify inconsistencies in issue type usage, label conventions, and component structures
  • Document findings for the JIRA recommendations report (target: Week 4)

Baseline Metrics

The Team Pulse Check and Leader Sentiment surveys are being sent this week as the engagement baseline. These will be re-administered approximately every 4–5 weeks to track trajectory. The Pulse Check captures team-level health signals (psychological safety, clarity, delivery confidence) while Leader Sentiment captures management-level alignment and engagement.
