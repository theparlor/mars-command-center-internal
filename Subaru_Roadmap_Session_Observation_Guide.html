Roadmap Planning Session — Observation Guide

Subaru M.A.R.S. · Agile Transformation Assessment ·
Who's in the Room (name / role-team / initial observations)
1. Priority & Decision Mechanics Intake · Backlog
Explicit How are items presented?
Slides with business cases? Jira epics on screen? A spreadsheet list? Verbal pitches? Watch what artifacts are used — this tells you the planning tool reality.
Maps to → jira_project_structure, intake_source, prioritization_method
Explicit Decision mechanism
Is there a framework (WSJF, RICE, MoSCoW)? Voting? Does one person just decide? Do they reference customer data or is it gut feel? Listen for "we need this because..." vs. "the business wants..."
Maps to → prioritization_method, intake_approval
Inferred Who has the real power?
Watch eye contact — who do people look at before committing? Who can veto? Who stays silent but whose silence blocks progress? HIPPO or distributed authority?
Maps to → role_product_lead, role_business_partners, decision_making
2. Capacity & Estimation Reality Estimation · Capacity
Explicit How is capacity expressed?
Story points? Sprints? "Developer-weeks"? T-shirt sizes? Or just "we can do it" / "we can't"? Is capacity data on screen or in someone's head?
Maps to → estimation_method, estimation_accuracy
Inferred Confidence vs. compliance
Do managers push back on scope? Or silently accept everything? Watch body language when commitments are made — crossed arms, hesitation, looking away. "We'll try" vs. "We will" is a crucial distinction.
Maps to → capacity_planning, estimation_accuracy
Explicit Are there numbers?
Velocity charts? Historical data? Burndown references? Or is everything aspirational? If numbers exist, do people trust them or argue about them?
Maps to → jira_reporting, estimation_accuracy
3. Cross-Team Dependencies Dependencies · Release
Explicit Are dependencies named?
Do managers say "we need X from [team]"? Are dependencies mapped visually? Or does someone discover a dependency mid-discussion and look surprised? Watch for the "oh wait, that depends on..." moments.
Maps to → dependency_identification, dependency_management
Inferred How are conflicts resolved?
When two teams need the same resource or have competing timelines, what happens? Escalation? Horse-trading? Avoidance? Does anyone own cross-team sequencing?
Maps to → dependency_management, cross_team_ceremonies
Inferred Shared Services & TechShare dynamics
How do shared teams (Giuffrida, Williams) negotiate capacity across their consumers? Do product teams assume availability or ask? Is there a queuing model?
Maps to → dependency_management, intake_source
4. Roles & Accountability (Live) Roles · New Category
Explicit Who speaks for each team?
Is it always the manager? Does a Scrum Lead or Engineering Lead present? Does anyone from the "business" side present alongside the team? Name every speaker and their role.
Maps to → role_scrum_lead, role_engineering_lead, role_product_lead
Inferred Product voice present or absent?
Is anyone articulating customer outcomes, success metrics, or "why this matters"? Or is the discussion purely about features and timelines? The absence of product voice is the loudest signal.
Maps to → role_product_lead, role_business_partners
Inferred Business stakeholder behavior
Are business partners in the room? Do they observe, participate, or drive? Do they bypass the process and lobby individual managers? Do they talk outcomes or features?
Maps to → role_business_partners, intake_source, intake_approval
5. Tooling Reality Check Jira · Tooling
Explicit What's on screen?
Jira boards? PowerPoint? Excel spreadsheets? Confluence? Miro? The tool that's on screen during planning is the tool that actually matters — regardless of what they say in interviews.
Maps to → jira_project_structure, jira_hierarchy (Outside Jira / Hybrid options)
Inferred Jira trust level
Does anyone say "let me check Jira" or do they check a spreadsheet? When data is questioned, what source do they trust? If Jira is on screen, do people agree with what it shows?
Maps to → jira_hygiene, jira_reporting
6. Communication & Process Signals Communication · Events
Explicit Language & vocabulary
Do they say "sprint" or "iteration" or "cycle"? "Story" or "ticket" or "task"? "Velocity" or "throughput"? Vocabulary reveals training lineage. Do teams use the same terms or different ones?
Maps to → overall agile maturity signal
Inferred Planning horizon & cadence
Are they planning by quarter? PI? Sprint? Is there a rhythm or does this feel ad hoc? How far out are they comfortable committing? The planning horizon reveals process maturity.
Maps to → sprint_cadence, refinement_practice
Inferred Information flow direction
Top-down ("here's what we're doing") vs. bottom-up ("here's what we can deliver") vs. collaborative? Does information flow through managers or do ICs participate? This tells you the team's actual operating model.
Maps to → decision_making, communication_channels
7. Release & Quality Signals Release · QA
Explicit Release constraints mentioned
Do teams mention release windows, code freezes, environment availability, or QA bottlenecks? These constraints reveal the real deployment pipeline regardless of what they tell you later.
Maps to → release_cadence, release_process, qa_integration
Inferred Technical debt posture
Does anyone advocate for tech debt, infrastructure, or "keep the lights on" work? Or is it all features? The ratio of new vs. sustain work in planning reveals engineering health.
Maps to → quality_ownership, pain_points
Gut Check — Capture Immediately After Session
What surprised you most?
What was conspicuously absent?
If you had to bet: is this org doing agile, or doing agile theater?
Which team(s) seem most/least mature based on this session?
