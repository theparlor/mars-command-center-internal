1. Design Philosophy

Consultant-Authored, Client-Branded

Turnberry's professional layout, typography, and information architecture form the chassis. Subaru's identity layers on as accents and highlights — not dominant chrome. Think McKinsey deck: our rigor and structure, your brand and context. This separation is intentional and applies to every format: HTML, PPTX, PDF, XLSX, email, and Miro.

Functional Colors Are Context, Not Brand

Service lane colors (Training, Coaching, Jira, OCM, Admin, Milestones) communicate what kind of work is being shown. They're independent of both Subaru blue and Turnberry teal. They never compete with brand — they carry meaning.

Internal vs. Client — One Glance

Client-facing work uses Subaru Blue (#00205B) accent bars, headings, and gradient footers. Internal-only documents swap to Amber (#E67E22). This makes every document instantly scannable: is this for the client or for our team?

Rich, Not Minimalist

Our PPTX and dashboard design vocabulary is deliberately rich — navy hero bands, colored top-band cards, icon circles, tinted row backgrounds, pill-shaped status badges, yellow callout boxes, and card shadows. This isn't decoration; it's information density through visual encoding. Every shape, color, and shadow carries meaning.

4g. Shadow Factory

All card shadows use the same factory function to avoid PptxGenJS object-reuse bugs:

mkShadow = () => ({ type: "outer", color: "000000", blur: 6, offset: 2, angle: 135, opacity: 0.07 })

Must create a fresh object each time — PptxGenJS mutates shadow options internally.

10b. Co-Branding Strategy

Why Co-Brand At All?

Every logo on a slide costs content real estate. The decision to consume that space must be intentional and earn its keep. Co-branding serves two audiences simultaneously: Subaru sees their brand dominant on materials about their transformation — signaling ownership, not something being done to them. Turnberry's presence — subtle but unmistakable — establishes IP attribution. When these decks travel through Subaru's organization (and they will), Turnberry's name travels with them. This is how consulting firms build referenceability.

The Height Budget Insight

On a 16:9 slide (10" × 5.63"), every 0.1" of vertical space = 1.8% of the canvas. The critical realization: logos consume width within existing structural bands, not additional height. The hero band (1.58") and footer (0.50") already exist in every build script. We're filling space that was previously underutilized, not adding new vertical elements. Net height cost: 0.00".

Anti-Patterns

What NOT to do:
• Dueling logos: Two logo images side-by-side in every footer. Creates visual competition in space that should be quiet.
• Logo soup: More than two logos on any single slide. Third parties get text attribution.
• Equal sizing: Both logos the same size signals ambiguity about whose program this is. Subaru should always be larger.
• Logo as decoration: Logos on slides that don't need them. Data slides earn their full real estate for content.
• Footer logo wars: Both logos as images in the footer. One image (Subaru) + one text (Turnberry) = clean.

11. PptxGenJS Technical Rules

Hard-won lessons from building 6 production PPTX scripts. These are non-negotiable.

RuleWhy
No "#" prefix on hex colorsPptxGenJS expects "00205B" not "#00205B"
Never reuse shadow/option objectsPptxGenJS mutates them internally. Use mkShadow() factory.
pres.shapes.RECTANGLE not pres.ShapeType.rectWrong API surface. Use the pres.shapes.* constants.
bullet: true not unicode bulletsPptxGenJS handles bullet rendering; unicode chars cause alignment issues.
breakLine: true for multi-line text arraysEach item in the text array needs this to get its own line.
Fresh mkShadow() per shapeCalling mkShadow() returns a new object each time. Never assign to a variable and reuse.
Icon pipeline: react-icons → ReactDOMServer → sharp → base64Only reliable way to get crisp vector-origin icons into PPTX.
pres.layout = "LAYOUT_16x9"Default for Turnberry-authored decks (OQ-1 DECIDED). Canvas is 10" × 5.63". Use 4:3 only for Subaru redistribution.
fontFace: 'Calibri' (body), 'Calibri Light' (headings)OQ-2 DECIDED. Arial is retired. All build scripts must use Calibri family.
